# MODULE 1: Understanding the Nigerian Real Estate Industry

## Handbook 7: Understanding Property Titles and Documentation

*"Documents do not guarantee ownership—but understanding them helps you make better decisions."*

### Opening Story
One afternoon, a young engineer came into our office. He placed a file on my desk and smiled confidently. 

*"Sir,"* he said, *"I'm not worried about this land because it has a C of O."*

I opened the file and looked through the documents. After asking a few questions, I realised something. He had never read the document. He didn't know whose name was on it. He didn't know where the land described in the document was located. He didn't know whether the seller was the person named in the document. 

He simply believed that because someone had said "C of O," everything was automatically safe. 

That day reminded me that documents don't protect people. **Knowledge protects people.** 

A document is only useful when you understand what it means, what it does, and what it *does not* do. That is why every Housmata Property Advisor must become confident in explaining property documentation. 

Not to practise law. Not to replace surveyors. But to educate clients and know when to involve the right professionals.

### Lesson 1: Why Property Documents Matter

Imagine buying a car without registration papers. Imagine opening a bank account without identification. Imagine buying shares without proof of ownership. 

Property works the same way. 

Documents help establish identity, ownership history, rights, boundaries, and the legal basis upon which property is held or transferred. 

No single document answers every question. Instead, different documents tell different parts of the property's story. Your role is to understand that story.

---

### Lesson 2: Survey Plan

**What is it?**
A Survey Plan is a document prepared by a licensed surveyor showing the exact location, dimensions, boundaries, shape, and coordinates of a piece of land. Think of it as the property's "map."

Without accurately identifying the land, it becomes difficult to know exactly what is being bought or sold. 

**Important Point:**
A Survey Plan identifies land. It does **not**, by itself, prove ownership. 

**How to Explain It to a Client:**
*"A Survey Plan helps us know exactly where the land is. It tells us the size, shape, and boundaries, but by itself it does not confirm who owns the land."*

---

### Lesson 3: Deed of Assignment

**What is it?**
A Deed of Assignment is a legal document used to transfer the seller's interest in a property to the buyer. 

In simple terms: If ownership is changing from one person to another, the Deed of Assignment records that transfer. It tells the story of who transferred what, to whom, and under what terms. 

**How to Explain It to a Client:**
*"The Deed of Assignment is the document that records the transfer of ownership rights from the seller to the buyer."*

---

### Lesson 4: Certificate of Occupancy (C of O)

**What is it?**
This is perhaps the most misunderstood property document in Nigeria. 

A Certificate of Occupancy is issued by the appropriate state government to recognise a person's or organisation's right to occupy and use land under the applicable land administration laws. 

It is an important document. But it is not magic. 
- It does **not** remove the need for proper due diligence. 
- It does **not** eliminate the need to confirm that the property being offered corresponds to the relevant documents. 
- Professional verification still matters. 

**How to Explain It to a Client:**
*"A Certificate of Occupancy is an important government-issued document recognising rights over land. It is one part of the verification process, not the entire process."*

---

### Lesson 5: Governor's Consent

**What is it?**
Sometimes a property that already has a Certificate of Occupancy is later transferred to another owner. Under Nigerian land administration practices, certain transfers require the Governor's Consent before they are fully regularised. 

As a Property Advisor, you are not expected to process these applications yourself. However, you should understand why clients may hear this term during a transaction and know when to direct them to the appropriate legal professionals. 

**Simple Explanation:**
*"Governor's Consent is generally part of the legal process for recognising certain transfers of interests in land after an earlier title has already been created."*

---

### Lesson 6: Excision

**What is it?**
This is another term that often confuses buyers. Historically, some land was acquired by government for public purposes. 

An Excision refers to land that has been released by government from such acquisition, allowing the relevant community or landowners to retain recognised interests, subject to applicable laws and procedures. 

Different states may have different administrative practices, so clients should always obtain appropriate professional advice regarding land under excision.

**Simple Explanation:**
*"Excision means government has recognised that a particular portion of land is no longer being held for public acquisition and has been released in accordance with the relevant legal process."*

---

### Lesson 7: Gazette

**What is it?**
A Gazette is an official government publication that records certain government decisions. 

When people refer to a "Gazette" in relation to land, they are usually referring to an official publication that documents matters such as approved excisions or other government actions. 

**Simple Explanation:**
*"A Gazette is an official government publication that records important government decisions relating to land."*

---

### Lesson 8: Allocation Letter

**What is it?**
An Allocation Letter is commonly issued by government agencies or estate developers to indicate that a particular plot or property has been allocated to a buyer or beneficiary. 

It is an important administrative document within many developments. However, its significance depends on the context of the transaction and should be considered alongside other relevant documentation (like a Deed or Survey).

**Simple Explanation:**
*"An Allocation Letter confirms that a particular plot has been assigned to you within an estate or allocation scheme."*

---

### Lesson 9: Receipt

**What is it?**
Many buyers underestimate the importance of receipts. A receipt confirms that payment has been made. 

It should clearly state:
- The payer.
- The payee.
- The amount paid.
- The purpose of payment.
- The date.
- The property reference where applicable.

A receipt is not a substitute for ownership documentation, but it forms an important part of the transaction record.

---

### Lesson 10: No Single Document Tells the Whole Story

One of the greatest mistakes buyers make is asking: *"Does this property have a C of O?"*

A better question is: *"Can we understand the complete documentation and verification history of this property?"*

Professional advice considers the full picture—not just one document. 

#### The Housmata Documentation Principle

At Housmata we teach: **Documents answer questions.** Different documents answer different questions. 

For example:
- **Where is the land?** -> Survey Plan
- **Who transferred the interest?** -> Deed of Assignment
- **Has a government title been issued?** -> Certificate of Occupancy (where applicable)
- **Has a recognised transfer process been completed where required?** -> Governor's Consent (where applicable)
- **Which plot has been allocated?** -> Allocation Letter
- **Was payment made?** -> Receipt

A Property Advisor's role is not to memorise documents. It is to understand **what question each document helps answer**.

### Case Study: Explaining Documentation

> [!NOTE]
> **Scenario:** Two advisors are asked whether a property is "safe" because it has a Certificate of Occupancy. 
>
> The first replies: *"Yes, it has a C of O, so there is nothing to worry about."*
>
> The second replies: *"A Certificate of Occupancy is an important document, but let's also review the survey, the chain of ownership, the seller's authority, and any other relevant information before reaching a conclusion."*
>
> **Outcome:** Which advisor demonstrates professional judgement? The answer is obvious. The second advisor protects the client from potential hidden issues.
