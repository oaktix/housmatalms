### Opening Story
In 2018, a gentleman walked into our office carrying a brown envelope. Inside the envelope were receipts, a survey plan, photographs of a piece of land, and several legal documents. 

He looked disappointed. Then he said something I will never forget. 

*"I have already paid for this land. I just want you to tell me whether I actually own it."*

Unfortunately, by the time he came to us, it was already too late. He had trusted the wrong people. He had never verified the ownership. He had never checked the title. He had never confirmed whether the seller had the legal right to sell. 

He bought first. He investigated later. By then, he had already lost millions of naira. 

As I listened to him, I realised something. The problem wasn't that Nigerians wanted to buy property. The problem was that many people simply didn't know **how to buy property safely**. 

That experience strengthened my conviction that every Property Advisor must become a guardian of trust.

### Lesson 1: Why Verification Matters

Imagine buying a car without checking who owns it. Imagine paying school fees into the wrong bank account because you never confirmed the account details. You would never do that. 

Yet many people commit to buying land worth millions of naira based on nothing more than a photograph, a phone call, or a promise. Property transactions deserve even greater care. 

Verification protects:
- **The buyer** from losing their money.
- **The seller** from legal disputes.
- **The Property Advisor** from destroying their reputation.
- **The developer** from partnering with fraudulent agents.
- **The lender** from financing a high-risk asset.
- **The Housmata brand** from association with unethical practices.

Every successful transaction begins with confidence, and confidence begins with verification.

---

### Lesson 2: Verification Is More Than Documents

Many new agents believe verification simply means asking to see a Certificate of Occupancy (C of O). That is only one part of the process. 

Verification means answering a series of important questions:
- Does this property actually exist?
- Can it be clearly identified on the ground?
- Does the seller have the legal authority to sell?
- Are the documents genuine?
- Is the land free from known disputes or encumbrances?
- Does the property's intended use align with planning regulations?
- Are there any risks the client should understand?

Verification is about gathering evidence—not making assumptions.

### Lesson 4: The Housmata VERIFY Framework

Every Property Advisor should use the **VERIFY Framework** before recommending a property.

**V – Vendor**
- Who is selling the property? 
- Are they the rightful owner? 
- A developer? 
- An authorised representative? 
- Can they demonstrate authority to sell?

**E – Existence**
- Does the property physically exist? 
- Has it been inspected? 
- Can its location be confirmed?

**R – Records**
- What documents are available? 
- Have they been reviewed? 
- Do they appear consistent? 
- Have relevant searches or checks been recommended where appropriate?

**I – Identity**
- Can the seller's identity be established? 
- If acting for someone else, is there clear authority (like a Power of Attorney) to do so?

**F – Facts**
- Do the photographs, descriptions, dimensions, and location accurately reflect the property? 
- Are there any material facts the client should know? 
*Transparency is part of professionalism.*

**Y – Your Professional Judgment**
After gathering the available information, ask yourself one final question: 
*"Would I recommend this property to a member of my own family?"*

If the answer is no—or if important questions remain unanswered—do not rush the transaction. **Pause. Investigate.**

---

### Lesson 5: Red Flags Every Property Advisor Should Recognise

While every situation is different, these warning signs should prompt additional caution:
- A seller insists on immediate payment without allowing reasonable verification.
- The asking price is significantly below comparable properties without a clear explanation.
- The information provided changes repeatedly.
- Different people claim ownership of the same property.
- Important documents cannot be produced or explained.
- The client is discouraged from seeking legal or professional advice.
- Pressure is used to force a quick decision.

A professional advisor does not ignore warning signs because of the possibility of earning a commission. Protect the client.

### Lesson 6: Verification Builds Trust

Many people think verification slows down transactions. In reality, verification speeds up confidence. 

A buyer who knows that proper checks have been carried out is more likely to proceed with peace of mind. A developer with transparent documentation is more likely to attract serious buyers. 

A Property Advisor known for careful verification earns referrals because clients know their interests come first. 

Trust is not created by advertising. Trust is created by consistent professional behaviour.
