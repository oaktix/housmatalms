# MODULE 1: Understanding the Nigerian Real Estate Industry

## Handbook 4: Why Property Values Increase

*"People don't make money simply because they bought land. They make money because they bought the right land, in the right place, at the right time."*

### Opening Story
In 2015, a client visited our office looking for land in Ibadan. 

After discussing his goals and budget, we recommended a location that was still largely undeveloped. There were few houses, untarred roads, and very little commercial activity. 

He smiled politely but said, *"Akin, this place is too far. Nothing is happening here."* 

Instead, he bought land in another location because it already looked developed. 

A few years later, major infrastructure projects transformed the area we had originally recommended. New roads were built. Residential estates sprang up. Commercial developments followed. Land values multiplied several times over. 

The client called me one afternoon. He laughed and said, *"I wasn't buying land. I was buying what I could see. You were buying what was coming."* 

That conversation taught me an important lesson: **Successful property investors don't just see today's landscape—they learn to recognise tomorrow's opportunities.**

---

### Learning Objectives
By the end of this handbook, you should be able to:
- Explain why property values change.
- Identify the key drivers of appreciation.
- Distinguish between speculation and informed investment.
- Recognise early signs of future growth.
- Guide clients using facts rather than assumptions.

---

### Lesson 1: Land Does Not Appreciate by Magic

One of the biggest misconceptions in Nigeria is the statement: *"Land never depreciates."* 

That statement is incomplete. Land has the *potential* to appreciate, but appreciation depends on several factors. Some land doubles in value within a few years. Some remains almost unchanged for decades. 

The difference is rarely luck. It is usually the result of location, demand, infrastructure, economic activity, and urban planning. 

As a Property Advisor, never promise clients that a property *will* appreciate by magic. Instead, help them understand the tangible factors that influence future value.

---

### Lesson 2: Infrastructure Creates Value

One of the strongest drivers of property appreciation is infrastructure. 

Examples include:
- New highways and expanded road networks.
- Bridges.
- Rail lines.
- Airports.
- Universities and educational institutions.
- Hospitals.
- Industrial parks.
- Government secretariats.
- Shopping centres.
- Power projects.

Infrastructure attracts people. People create demand. Demand increases property values. This is why experienced investors pay close attention to government development plans and major private-sector projects before they are fully visible.

---

### Lesson 3: Population Growth

People need places to live, work, learn, shop, and worship. As populations grow, demand for land and buildings generally increases. 

Cities experiencing rapid population growth often require:
- More housing.
- More schools.
- More hospitals.
- More offices.
- More retail space.
- Better transport.

Where demand grows faster than supply, property values almost always rise.

---

### Lesson 4: Economic Activity

Property performs best where people can earn a living. 

Ask yourself:
- Are businesses moving into the area?
- Are industries being established?
- Are jobs being created?
- Are people relocating there for work?

Economic activity attracts residents, investors, and developers. An area without economic opportunities may struggle to sustain long-term property demand.

---

### Lesson 5: Government Planning Matters

Many successful investors study government plans before buying land. Important questions include:
- Is the area within an approved development plan?
- Are major roads proposed?
- Is the land affected by future public projects (e.g., a proposed airport)?
- Are there zoning restrictions?
- Are there environmental concerns?

Professional advice is based on research, not rumours.

---

### Lesson 6: Supply and Demand

Like every market, property values are heavily influenced by supply and demand. 

When many people want to buy property in a location where few plots are available, prices often increase. Conversely, when supply greatly exceeds demand (e.g., too many luxury apartments in an area with few high-income earners), prices may remain stable or even decline. 

Understanding this principle helps advisors explain market behaviour to clients without making unrealistic promises.

---

### Lesson 7: Reputation and Confidence

Trust also affects value. 

Properties developed by reputable companies with proper documentation and a history of delivering on their promises often attract stronger demand and command premium prices. 

Similarly, locations known for secure ownership records and organised developments tend to inspire greater buyer confidence. Confidence encourages investment.

---

### Lesson 8: The Danger of Speculation

Not every story of rapid appreciation will repeat itself. A professional Property Advisor must distinguish between:
- **Evidence-based opportunity:** Supported by infrastructure, planning, and demand.
- **Speculation:** Based solely on rumours, social media hype, or unrealistic promises.

Never encourage clients to invest just because "everyone is buying there." Encourage them to invest because the *facts* support the opportunity.

---

### The Housmata GROWTH Analysis Framework

Before recommending land as an investment, every Housmata Property Advisor should ask:

**G – Government Plans**
Are there approved public projects or policies that may influence growth?

**R – Road and Infrastructure**
Are there existing or planned roads, utilities, schools, hospitals, or transport links?

**O – Occupancy**
Are people already moving into the area? Are homes and businesses being developed?

**W – Wealth Drivers**
What economic activities are creating jobs and attracting investment?

**T – Titles and Transparency**
Can the property be properly verified? Are the ownership and documentation clear?

**H – Horizon**
What is the expected investment timeframe? Is the client prepared to hold the investment if necessary?

This **GROWTH Framework** is a practical tool you must apply throughout your career.

### Case Study: Evidence vs. Rumour

> [!NOTE]
> **Scenario:** Two investors each have ₦15 million. 
>
> Investor A buys land because a friend says, *"Prices will triple next year."*
>
> Investor B researches the area. She studies proposed infrastructure, verifies the title, understands the development timeline, and aligns the investment with her long-term goals using the GROWTH Framework.
>
> **Outcome:** Five years later, Investor B understands exactly why her investment performed the way it did, while Investor A still relies on rumours to make decisions and is disappointed by stagnant growth.
>
> **Lesson:** The difference is not intelligence. The difference is discipline.

### Chapter Summary

At the end of this chapter, you should understand that:
- Property values increase because of identifiable economic and development factors, not magic.
- Infrastructure, population, economic activity, planning, and confidence all influence demand.
- Great Property Advisors rely on research rather than rumours.
- The Housmata GROWTH Framework provides a structured way to evaluate investment opportunities.
- Your credibility depends on giving balanced, evidence-based advice rather than making promises about future returns.
