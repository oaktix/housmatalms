# MODULE 1: Understanding the Nigerian Real Estate Industry

## Handbook 5: Understanding the Nigerian Property Buyer

*"People don't buy land. They buy dreams, security, opportunity, and peace of mind."*

### Opening Story
One Saturday morning, two clients walked into our office only thirty minutes apart. 

The first client was a young medical doctor. He wanted to buy land in Ibadan. 

The second client was also interested in buying land in Ibadan. 

If you listened only to those two sentences, you would think they wanted exactly the same thing. But after spending time with each of them, I discovered something completely different. 

The doctor planned to build a home within two years. He wanted good roads, electricity, security, schools for his children, and a peaceful environment. 

The second client lived in the United Kingdom. He had no intention of building for at least fifteen years. His only goal was long-term capital appreciation. 

Both wanted land. But they were buying for completely different reasons. If I had recommended the same property to both clients, I would have failed one of them. 

That day reminded me of a lesson that has shaped my career: **Never recommend a property until you understand the person behind the purchase.**

### Lesson 1: Every Buyer Has a Story

One of the biggest mistakes inexperienced agents make is assuming that every buyer is looking for the cheapest property or the biggest discount. 

The truth is that people buy property for deeply personal reasons. 
- Some are trying to fulfil a lifelong dream. 
- Others are planning for retirement. 
- Some want to leave something valuable for their children. 
- Others simply want a place they can finally call home. 

Behind every purchase is a story. Your responsibility is to discover that story.

---

### Lesson 2: The Six Types of Property Buyers

Although every client is unique, most buyers fall into one of these six categories.

#### 1. The Home Builder
This buyer wants somewhere to build and live. Their concerns include:
- Safety.
- Good roads.
- Electricity.
- Water.
- Schools.
- Hospitals.
- Community.
- Future family needs.
*They are buying a lifestyle.*

#### 2. The Investor
This buyer is interested in returns. Questions they ask include:
- How fast will the area develop?
- What is the expected appreciation?
- Is there future demand?
- What infrastructure is coming?
- Can I resell easily?
*They are buying future value.*

#### 3. The Diaspora Buyer
This client is often thousands of kilometres away. Their greatest concern is rarely price. It is **trust**. They ask questions like:
- Can I trust this developer?
- Can I trust this advisor?
- Is the land genuine?
- Can somebody inspect it for me?
- Will my documents be safe?
*Diaspora buyers don't just buy property. They buy confidence.*

#### 4. The Business Buyer
This buyer wants property that supports business growth. They care about:
- Customer access.
- Traffic.
- Parking.
- Visibility.
- Logistics.
- Commercial viability.

#### 5. The Retirement Buyer
This client is planning for later life. They want:
- Peace.
- Accessibility.
- Healthcare.
- Security.
- Stable communities.

#### 6. The Legacy Buyer
Some clients aren't buying for themselves. They're buying for:
- Their children.
- Future generations.
- Family wealth.
*Their thinking is long-term.*

### Lesson 3: The Five Questions Every Advisor Must Ask

Never recommend property before asking these questions:

**Question One: Why are you buying?**
This single question changes everything.

**Question Two: When do you intend to use the property?**
Immediately? Two years? Ten years? Never?

**Question Three: What is your budget?**
Don't assume. Ask respectfully.

**Question Four: How do you intend to pay?**
Cash? Installments? Mortgage? Employer Scheme? Housing Cooperative? Developer Payment Plan? 
*This question becomes especially important within the Housmata ecosystem because financing options may be available through our approved partners.*

**Question Five: What would make this purchase successful for you?**
This question uncovers their deepest expectations.

### Lesson 4: Listening Is More Valuable Than Talking

Many agents interrupt clients. Professional advisors observe. 

Listen for:
- Fear.
- Excitement.
- Confusion.
- Urgency.
- Family concerns.
- Investment goals.
- Financial limitations.

People often tell you exactly what they need. If you listen carefully.

### Lesson 5: The Housmata Discovery Conversation

Every Housmata Advisor should follow a structured client discovery process before recommending any property. We call it the **DISCOVER Framework**.

**D – Dream**
What is the client's vision? What are they trying to achieve?

**I – Investment Goal**
Home? Rental income? Land banking? Commercial use? Legacy?

**S – Spending Capacity**
What is the realistic budget? How will payment be made? Cash? Installments? Potential mortgage?

**C – Confidence**
What concerns does the client have? Fraud? Documentation? Location? Developer credibility?

**O – Occupation & Lifestyle**
What does the client do? Do they travel often? Do they work remotely? Do they have children? Are they in Nigeria or the diaspora? These answers influence recommendations.

**V – Vision Timeline**
When will they build? When do they expect returns? How long can they hold the investment?

**E – Expectations**
What does success look like for this client?

*Only after completing the DISCOVER conversation should a Housmata Advisor recommend a property.*

---

### Lesson 6: Never Sell from Your Pocket

This is one of the most common mistakes among new advisors. 

A client says: *"I have ₦20 million."* 

The advisor immediately starts showing ₦20 million properties. 

Wrong. 

A professional first asks: *"What are you trying to accomplish?"* 

Sometimes the best recommendation costs less than the client's maximum budget. Your responsibility is to solve the client's problem—not to spend their money.

### Case Study: Advising vs. Selling

> [!NOTE]
> **Scenario:** Mrs. Ada lives in the United States and contacts a Housmata Property Advisor about a plot of land she saw online. 
>
> Instead of immediately sending payment details, the advisor begins by asking questions about her goals, budget, timeline, and concerns. During the conversation, the advisor learns that Mrs. Ada intends to build a retirement home within three years and may need financing for construction in the future. 
>
> **Outcome:** Rather than recommending the original property, the advisor suggests another verified location with better infrastructure, easier access, and stronger long-term suitability for her plans. The advisor also explains how Housmata's finance partners may be able to assist when she is ready to begin construction. 
>
> **Lesson:** Mrs. Ada later says: *"For the first time, I felt like someone was advising me instead of trying to sell to me."* That is the Housmata difference.
