# MODULE 14: Running Your Property Business

## Handbook 2: CRM & KPI Management

*"If you cannot measure it, you cannot manage it."* — Peter Drucker

### Opening Story
An advisor worked 12 hours a day, constantly busy with phone calls, WhatsApp messages, and site visits. Despite his exhaustion, his sales were low. When his business mentor asked him: *"What is your lead conversion rate? What is your customer acquisition cost? Where are you losing leads in your sales pipeline?"* 

The advisor could not answer. He was operating on feeling, not data. He was busy, but he was not productive.

Another advisor worked 6 hours a day. She tracked her business metrics in her CRM:
- She knew that out of every 100 leads generated, 20 booked inspections (20% booking rate).
- Out of 20 inspections, 2 closed (10% closing rate).
- Her Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) was ₦25,000 per closed sale.

With this data, she knew that to earn three commissions next month, she needed to generate exactly 150 leads. She focused her time and marketing budget on generating those 150 leads, closed the sales, and went home to rest.

She worked less and earned more because she managed her business using KPIs.

### Lesson 1: The Sales Pipeline Stages

Your sales pipeline is the path a client takes from their first inquiry to closing the purchase. You must track every lead's stage in the CRM:

1. **Lead Generated (New Inquiry):** Contact info captured. No profiling yet.
2. **Profiled (Contacted):** Budget, location preference, and timeline confirmed.
3. **Advisory (Recommendations Sent):** Handbooks, due diligence reports, or portfolios sent.
4. **Site Inspection:** The physical tour of the property or coordinates check.
5. **Negotiation / Offer:** Offer Letter sent or payment terms being discussed.
6. **Closed Won (Paid):** Deposit or full payment made, contract executed.
7. **Closed Lost:** The lead dropped out or bought elsewhere.

*Rule:* If you don't know which stage a lead is in, your pipeline is unmanaged.

---

### Lesson 2: Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

To run a professional property advisory business, you must track these four metrics:

#### 1. Lead Volume & Cost Per Lead (CPL)
The total number of inquiries generated and the marketing cost to acquire one inquiry.
$$\text{Cost Per Lead (CPL)} = \frac{\text{Total Marketing Ad Spend}}{\text{Total Leads Generated}}$$

#### 2. Conversion Rate (Lead-to-Sale)
The percentage of leads that actually purchase a property.
$$\text{Conversion Rate (\%)} = \left( \frac{\text{Total Closed Sales}}{\text{Total Leads Generated}} \right) \times 100$$
A healthy conversion rate in property advisory ranges between 1% to 3%.

#### 3. Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
The total marketing and sales expenditure required to acquire one paying customer.
$$\text{Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)} = \frac{\text{Total Marketing + Sales Expenses}}{\text{Total Closed Customers}}$$

#### 4. Sales Cycle Length
The average number of days it takes for a lead to transition from Stage 1 (New Inquiry) to Stage 6 (Paid). Typically ranges from 30 to 90 days for property transactions.

---

### Lesson 3: Auditing Pipeline Leaks

A **pipeline leak** occurs when leads drop out at a specific stage. By auditing your CRM data, you can identify and fix these leaks:

- **High Lead Volume, Low Booking Rate:** If you have 500 leads but only 2 book inspections, your lead quality is low (bad ad targeting) or your follow-up speed is too slow.
- **High Inspection Volume, Low Closing Rate:** If you take 30 clients to the site but none buy, either the property is defective, the price is misaligned, or your objection-handling skills on-site are weak.
- **High Offer Rate, Low Closing Rate:** If clients sign Offer Letters but fail to make payments, your closing sequence is disorganized or the payment plans are too rigid.

---

### Case Study: Fixing the Inspection Leak

> [!NOTE]
> **Scenario:** Advisor Wale generated 100 leads monthly from Facebook ads. He booked 30 inspections, but closed zero sales over three months. He was spending ₦150,000 monthly on transport and ad spend, and was losing money.
> 
> **The Audit:** Under review, his CRM logs showed that during inspections, most clients complained that the access road was bad. Wale was trying to sell raw plots to clients who wanted to build immediately within 12 months.
> 
> **The Correction:** Wale updated his ad targeting to focus strictly on "Long-term Land Bankers (5-10 year horizon)." 
> 
> **Outcome:** His next 10 inspections were with clients who understood the long-term timeline. He closed three sales within a month, recovering his investment.
> 
> **Lesson:** Aligning your client's timeline with the property's development stage prevents wasted inspections.

### Chapter Summary
- Managing a property agency requires tracking metrics: CPL, Conversion Rates, CAC, and Cycle Length.
- The CRM sales pipeline organizes client progress from lead to paid closing.
- Pipeline audits identify leaks (e.g., weak follow-up, bad targeting, poor on-site closing).
- Data-driven decisions prevent marketing budget waste and improve closing rates.
