# MODULE 5: Property Verification & Due Diligence

## Handbook 4: Developer, Seller Verification & Risk Reporting

*"A transaction is only as reliable as the people behind it."*

### Opening Story
In 2021, an investor paid ₦45 million to a developer for a 3-bedroom off-plan apartment in Lekki, Lagos. The developer promised to deliver the apartment in 12 months. The marketing brochure showed 3D renders of a luxury estate with a gym, swimming pool, and round-the-clock power supply.

Eighteen months later, the site remained at the foundation stage. The developer stopped responding to emails and eventually shut down his office.

Investigation revealed the developer had launched four different estate projects simultaneously using buyers' deposits, without having any capital of his own or bank financing. When construction costs rose due to inflation, the entire business collapsed. The developer had no assets in his corporate name, leaving the buyers with nothing but useless paper receipts.

This failure could have been avoided by verifying the developer's financial capacity and track record.

### Lesson 1: Deconstructing Developer Auditing

When advising clients on off-plan properties (buying before or during construction) or private estates, you must conduct a thorough audit of the developer. You are not just buying land; you are buying the developer's ability to execute:

#### 1. Corporate Registration & Share Capital (CAC Audit)
Confirm the developer is registered with the Corporate Affairs Commission (CAC). Look at the status (active or inactive). A company with a share capital of ₦1 million trying to build a ₦10 billion estate is under-capitalized and carries high default risk.

#### 2. Project Delivery Track Record
Visit the developer's completed estates. Talk to current residents and estate association chairpersons. Ask:
- *Did the developer deliver on the timeline promised?*
- *Are the drainage, electricity, and water systems working, or did they break down after six months?*
- *Did the developer deliver the promised amenities (e.g., gym, paved roads), or did they abandon them?*

#### 3. Land Security
Verify if the developer owns the land or has entered into a Joint Venture (JV). If it is a JV, audit the Deed of Joint Venture to ensure the landowner has signed off and the title is free from disputes.

#### 4. Financing Structure
Ask the developer: *"How is this project funded?"* If the project relies 100% on buyers' deposits (pre-sales), it is high risk. If sales slow down, construction stops. A reliable developer should have standby credit lines or significant equity capital to complete the structure.

### Lesson 2: Seller Verification

When buying from an individual seller (secondary market), you must confirm their identity and capacity to sell.

#### Essential Verification Checklist:
- **Identity Verification:** Confirm the seller's identity using government-issued IDs (NIN, International Passport, Driver's License) that match the name on the title.
- **Probate & Administration:** If the original owner is deceased, the property cannot be sold by children or relatives without a **Grant of Probate** or **Letters of Administration** issued by the High Court Probate Registry.
- **Spousal/Family Consent:** If the property is a family asset, ensure all principal family members (or both spouses) sign the consent to sale. Family land requires signatures from the Family Head and Principal Members.

### Lesson 3: The Due Diligence & Risk Report

Once verification is complete, a Housmata Advisor compiles all findings into a **Due Diligence & Risk Report**. This report is presented to the client to guide their decision.

#### Due Diligence Report Template Structure:

```markdown
# HOUSMATA PROPERTY DUE DILIGENCE REPORT

**Property Name/Location:** [e.g., Plot 12, Block 4, Meadow View Estate, Epe]
**Date of Report:** [Date]
**Lead Advisor:** [Your Name, HCPA]

---

### 1. Executive Status
- **Recommendation:** [APPROVED / APPROVED WITH CONDITIONS / REJECTED]
- **Risk Rating:** [LOW / MEDIUM / HIGH]

---

### 2. Title Audit (Pillar 1)
- **Presented Document:** [e.g., C of O, Deed of Assignment, Excision]
- **Registry Search Status:** [Verified at Lands Registry / Pending / Failed]
- **Easting/Northing Coordinate Search:** [Free from Acquisition / Committed / Excised]
- **Encumbrance Check:** [No registered mortgages or court disputes found]

---

### 3. Site & Physical Audit (Pillar 2)
- **Topography:** [e.g., 100% Dry land, level with road / Requires 2 feet sand-filling]
- **Infrastructure Access:** [Graded road, electricity poles present]
- **Setback Checks:** [Complies with road and power line setbacks]

---

### 4. Seller & Developer Audit (Pillars 3 & 4)
- **Seller Identity:** [Verified via NIN check]
- **Developer Profile:** [e.g., Active CAC registration, 3 successfully completed projects in Lekki]

---

### 5. Risk Assessment & Recommendations
- **Identified Risks:** [e.g., High-tension cable setback is close to the rear boundary]
- **Mitigation Action Required:** [e.g., Erect boundary wall 5 meters away from the rear beacon]
```
