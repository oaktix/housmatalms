# MODULE 15: Becoming a Housmata Certified Property Advisor

## Handbook 1: Certification Assessments & Onboarding

*"The path to certification is narrow, but the rewards are vast."*

### Opening Story
A candidate applied to join the Housmata network as a Certified Advisor. He had years of experience in another brokerage and had closed many deals. He assumed that because of his past sales, the Housmata certification would be a formality. He skipped the verification modules and did not practice coordinate checks.

During the final practical evaluation, he was presented with a mock property title. 

He failed to notice that the coordinates on the survey plan fell within a government agricultural reserve, and he approved the transaction.

The panel rejected his application.

The examiner noted: *"Real estate is not just about sales; it is about protection. A Housmata Advisor who cannot protect their client is not ready to carry our brand."*

He went back, studied the verification framework, and retook the assessment two months later, passing with excellence.

### Lesson 1: The Five Graduation Assessments

To earn the **Housmata Certified Property Advisor (HCPA)** designation, you must pass five assessments:

#### 1. The Ethical Conduct Evaluation (Ethics Check)
A case-study-based assessment where you must demonstrate how you handle conflicts of interest, high-commission dilemmas, and hidden property defects.

#### 2. The Client Consultation Simulation (Consultation Check)
A practical roleplay where you must profile a mock buyer, calculate their Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio, and recommend the correct property category based on their goals.

#### 3. The Digital Platform & CRM Assessment (Technology Check)
A hands-on test where you must demonstrate your ability to log communication logs, filter databases, and manage a mock transaction workflow in the CRM.

#### 4. The Site Verification Check (Verification Check)
A field evaluation where you must stand on a plot of land, pick GPS coordinates using the app, cross-reference them with the Surveyor General's records, and compile a Due Diligence Report.

#### 5. The Sales & Closing Simulation (Sales Check)
A roleplay evaluating your ability to handle client objections on-site and structure a property Offer Letter and Contract of Sale.

---

### Lesson 2: The Portfolio Binder Submission

Your final graduation requirement is the submission of a physical and digital **Transaction Portfolio Binder**.

#### Binder Contents:
- **Case 1 (Land Banking):** A complete Investment Advisory Report for a mock client looking for capital appreciation in Epe or Ibadan.
- **Case 2 (Residential Purchase):** An affordability and mortgage readiness plan for a salary earner buying a townhouse.
- **Case 3 (Due Diligence):** A verified coordinates check and due diligence report for an active listing in your area.
- **Ethics Pledge:** The signed Housmata Oath of Professional Conduct.

*Submission Format:* Upload all files as a single PDF binder via the Academy portal, and submit the physical copy to the regional training center.

---

### Lesson 3: The Graduation Board Review

Once your assessments are completed and your binder is submitted, the **Housmata Academy Board** will conduct a final review of your candidate file.

- **The Review Panel:** Composed of a senior lawyer, a licensed surveyor, and a partner advisor.
- **The Decision:** Your application will be approved, deferred for corrections, or rejected.
- **Onboarding Induction:** Approved candidates attend the induction ceremony, where they take the professional oath and receive their credentials kit.

---

### Case Study: The Deferred Binder

> [!NOTE]
> **Scenario:** Candidate Ayo submitted his final transaction portfolio binder. During the review, the board noticed that Ayo had omitted the valuation fee (0.5%) from his Cash-to-Close calculations in Case 2.
> 
> **Action:** Instead of rejecting him, the board deferred his certification and issued a feedback report: *"Please correct the cash-to-close metrics in Case 2 to include the mandatory valuation fee, and re-submit your binder within 7 days."*
> 
> **Outcome:** Ayo updated the calculations, re-submitted the binder, and was approved for induction.
> 
> **Lesson:** The board values precision. Even a minor omission in financial calculations can lead to client disputes in real transactions.

### Chapter Summary

- Approved candidates are formally inducted into the network after taking the professional oath.
