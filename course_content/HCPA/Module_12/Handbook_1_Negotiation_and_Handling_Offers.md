# MODULE 12: Negotiation & Closing

## Handbook 1: Negotiation & Handling Offers

*"Negotiation is not a battle to be won; it is a puzzle to be solved together."*

### Opening Story
An agent negotiated the sale of a house in Lekki. The seller wanted ₦90 million, and the buyer was willing to pay ₦85 million. The agent focused solely on the price, constantly calling both parties to push them to compromise: *"Please, sir, meet in the middle at ₦87.5 million."* 

Both parties became annoyed and felt pressured. The seller felt the agent was working for the buyer, and the buyer felt the agent was working for the seller. The deal collapsed, and both walked away.

A Housmata Certified Advisor took over the transaction. Instead of pushing on price, the advisor sat with the buyer and discovered they needed a flexible payment plan over 6 months because their capital was tied in corporate investments. He then spoke to the seller and learned they needed a cash deposit of ₦40 million immediately to secure another development project, but did not mind waiting for the balance.

**The Solution:** The advisor structured an offer: ₦88 million total price (closer to the seller's ask), but with a payment plan: ₦45 million immediate deposit (solving the seller's urgent need) and the balance of ₦43 million spread over 6 months (solving the buyer's liquidity issue).

Both parties signed immediately. The advisor closed the deal because he negotiated the **terms**, not just the price.

### Lesson 1: Principled Real Estate Negotiation

In traditional real estate, negotiation is often "positional" (buyer starts low, seller starts high, and they argue until one gives in). At Housmata, we apply **Principled Negotiation** (focusing on interests, not positions):

- **Separate the People from the Problem:** Never argue or become emotional. Keep the focus on resolving the financial and logistical barriers of the transaction.
- **Focus on Interests, Not Positions:** The buyer's *position* is "I want to pay ₦80 million." Their *interest* might be preserving cash flow. The seller's *position* is "I want ₦90 million." Their *interest* might be clearing a bank loan.
- **Invent Options for Mutual Gain:** Look for trade-offs: payment flexibility, handover dates, furniture inclusions, development levies, or title registration costs.

---

### Lesson 2: Writing & Presenting Offers

A professional offer must be documented. Never present an offer verbally or via a casual WhatsApp text. Use the formal **Housmata Offer Letter (Letter of Intent)**.

#### Key Components of a Written Offer:
1. **The Purchase Price:** The proposed price.
2. **Payment Terms:** Outline the deposit amount and the specific payment schedule for the balance.
3. **Conditions of Purchase:** Specify what must happen for the deal to close (e.g., *"Subject to a clean registry search"* or *"Subject to verification of boundaries"*).
4. **Validity Period:** Give the seller a clear deadline to accept or reject the offer (typically 3 to 5 business days). This creates a sense of urgency.

---

### Lesson 3: Handling Counter-Offers & Price Deadlocks

When a seller receives an offer, they will often issue a **Counter-Offer**. As an advisor, you must guide your client to evaluate the counter-offer calmly.

#### Strategies to Break a Price Deadlock:
- **Adjust the Payment Plan:** If the seller refuses to drop the price, ask for longer payment terms (e.g., extending from 3 months to 6 or 12 months). This increases the buyer's purchasing power.
- **Leverage Development Levies:** In estate purchases, negotiate the inclusion of development and documentation fees (which can add 10% to the cost) into the main purchase price.
- **Offer a Quick Close:** If the buyer has ready cash, offer to close the transaction and pay the full balance within 7 days in exchange for a price discount. Sellers value cash certainty.

### Case Study: The Levy Trade-off

> [!NOTE]
> **Scenario:** A buyer wanted to purchase a plot in a premium estate. The estate developer was firm on the price of ₦15 million. The buyer's maximum budget was ₦15 million, and he could not afford the additional ₦1.5 million required for development and documentation levies. The transaction was stuck.
> 
> **The Advisor's Intervention:** The advisor met with the developer: *"My client is ready to pay the ₦15 million in full today, but he has a strict capital limit. If you can waive or defer 50% of the development levy to next year, we can close the transaction immediately."*
> 
> **Outcome:** The developer agreed to defer the development levy until the buyer was ready to start construction in 12 months. The buyer signed, paid the ₦15 million, and secured the plot.
> 
> **Lesson:** When price cannot move, look at levies, fees, and timelines to find the middle ground.

---

### Chapter Summary
- Professional negotiation focuses on the interests and needs of both parties, not just price.
- All offers must be presented formally in writing using an Offer Letter with a validity deadline.
- Counter-offers should be handled by adjusting variables such as payment plans, closing speed, and levy waivers.
- Advisors manage negotiations to ensure both parties feel they have achieved a fair value.
