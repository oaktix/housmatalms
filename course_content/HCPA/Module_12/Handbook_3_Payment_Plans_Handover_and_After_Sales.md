# MODULE 12: Negotiation & Closing

## Handbook 3: Payment Plans, Handover & After-Sales

*"A great advisor is not remembered for how they sold the property, but for how they handed it over."*

### Opening Story
A buyer purchased a luxury apartment off-plan. She paid the ₦60 million in full over 12 months. On the day of handover, she arrived at the site with her family, excited to move in. 

However, the developer had not hooked up the electricity, the water pump was not connected, the paintwork was unfinished, and the kitchen cabinet was missing handles. The site manager handed her the keys and said: *"Don't worry, ma, we will fix those minor things next week."*

The buyer moved in. The developer never returned. For six months, she had to pay out of her own pocket to complete the repairs and hook up the utilities. 

A transaction that started with joy ended in deep bitterness. She told all her friends to avoid the developer and the agent who sold her the apartment.

### Lesson 1: Structuring Payment Plans

Many buyers cannot pay 100% upfront. To close more deals, you must master the **Installment Payment Plan**.

#### Key Payment Plan Structures:
1. **Outright Payment (0 - 3 Months):** Usually carries the lowest price/discount.
2. **Milestone-Based Payments:** Common in off-plan developments. Payments are tied to physical construction stages (e.g., 30% on foundation, 40% on roofing/carcass, 30% on finishing).
3. **Structured Installments (6 - 12+ Months):** The balance is divided into fixed monthly or quarterly payments.

#### Rules for Installment Management:
- **Default Clauses:** Ensure the Contract of Sale clearly states what happens if the buyer misses a payment (e.g., a grace period of 14 days, followed by a minor penalty interest rate or re-allocation of plots).
- **Payment tracking:** Update the client's financial ledger in the CRM immediately upon payment receipt.

### Lesson 2: The Handover Protocol

The **Handover Protocol** is the official process of transferring physical possession of the property from the seller/developer to the buyer. It must be conducted with high discipline:

#### Steps in the Handover Protocol:
1. **The Pre-Handover Punch List Inspection:** Before signing any handover document, walk the property with the client and a maintenance inspector. Create a **Punch List** of all unfinished items (e.g., scratches on doors, electrical outlets not working, plumbing leaks).
2. **Defect Resolution:** The developer must fix all items on the punch list before the final handover keys are issued.
3. **The Handover Certificate:** Once all repairs are verified, both parties sign the **Handover Certificate**, confirming the property has been delivered to standard.
4. **Document Package Exchange:** Hand over the original physical copies of:
   - Signed Deeds of Assignment.
   - Allocation Letter.
   - Original survey plan.
   - Keys and utility meters registration cards.

### Lesson 3: The After-Sales Relationship

Your career growth as a Property Advisor is built on repeat business. After-sales care is where you prove you are a partner, not a salesperson.

#### After-Sales Touchpoints:
- **Day 7 Post-Handover:** Call the client to check if they have settled in. Ask: *"Is the water supply working fine? Have you registered with the estate security office?"*
- **Day 90 Post-Handover:** Send a branded gift or a card. Ask if they have any friends looking to purchase in the same estate.
- **Ongoing Portfolio Updates:** Send annual appreciation reports showing them how much their asset has grown since purchase.

### Case Study: The Post-Closing Shield

> [!NOTE]
> **Scenario:** Mr. Tony bought a plot in an estate through Advisor Yusuf. One year after the purchase, the estate developer changed the layout, affecting Mr. Tony's plot boundary.
> 
> Instead of telling Mr. Tony to "resolve it with the developer," Yusuf stepped in:
> 1. He visited the site, picked the new coordinates, and cross-referenced them with the original survey.
> 2. He met with the developer's layout engineer and negotiated the allocation of a premium corner-piece plot of equivalent size as compensation.
> 
> **Outcome:** Mr. Tony did not experience any stress. The developer corrected the boundary, and Yusuf secured Mr. Tony's trust.
> 
> **Result:** Two months later, Mr. Tony's brother contacted Yusuf to invest ₦45 million, stating: *"My brother said you never abandon your clients."*
> 
> **Lesson:** Your service after the sale is your most effective marketing tool.
