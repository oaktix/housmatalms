# MODULE 6: Mortgage Readiness & Home Financing

## Handbook 3: Affordability Calculations & Lender Prep

*"It's not about what the house costs. It's about what you can afford to pay every month."*

### Opening Story
An excited buyer negotiated a deal to purchase a townhouse in Lekki for ₦70 million. He qualified for a mortgage with a bank that offered a 70% Loan-to-Value (LTV). The buyer was thrilled—he had the ₦21 million (30% equity) ready in his account. He signed the contract and paid a non-refundable deposit.

Two weeks later, the bank issued the offer letter. The buyer realized he had overlooked the **additional closing costs**. He was required to pay:
- ₦3.5 million (5% Governor's Consent & registration fees)
- ₦1.4 million (2% Legal fees)
- ₦1.4 million (2% Agency fee)
- ₦500,000 (Valuation and administrative fees)
- ₦1.0 million (Credit insurance)

In total, he needed an extra ₦7.8 million in cash before the bank would release the loan. Because he had spent his entire savings on the ₦21 million equity contribution, he could not pay the closing fees. The transaction collapsed, and he forfeited a portion of his deposit.

This financial disaster could have been prevented by a comprehensive affordability calculation.

### Lesson 1: The Debt-to-Income (DTI) Cap

Lenders do not want borrowers to default on their food, school fees, or medical bills to pay their mortgage. Therefore, they apply the **Debt-to-Income (DTI) Ratio** rule.

In Nigeria, most lenders cap the DTI at **33% to 40%** of the applicant's verifiable net monthly income.

#### The Risk of Over-Leveraging:
If a client earns ₦1 million net monthly, and their mortgage repayment is ₦500,000, their DTI is 50%. The bank will reject this because any increase in interest rates or sudden personal emergencies would cause the borrower to default. You must calculate the DTI cap before the client starts looking at houses, so they only shop within their actual budget.

#### How to Calculate Maximum Monthly Repayment:
$$\text{Max Monthly Repayment} = \text{Net Monthly Income} \times 0.35$$

*Example:* If a client earns ₦1.5 million net monthly salary:
$$\text{Max Monthly Repayment} = 1,500,000 \times 0.35 = ₦525,000$$

If the interest rate and tenure of the loan result in a monthly repayment of ₦600,000, the bank will reject the loan—even if the client has the equity contribution—because it violates the DTI limit.

### Lesson 2: Total Cash-to-Close Calculations

When buying property via a mortgage, the buyer needs more than just the equity contribution. They must have the **Total Cash-to-Close**.

As a Housmata Advisor, you must calculate the total cash required using this formula:

$$\text{Total Cash-to-Close} = \text{Equity Contribution} + \text{Closing Costs}$$

#### Breakdown of Standard Closing Costs in Nigeria:
1. **Consent & Registration Fees (Governor's Consent):** 3% to 6% of the property value (paid to the state government to register the new transfer).
2. **Legal / Documentation Fee:** 1% to 2% (paid to the property lawyer).
3. **Agency/Brokerage Fee:** 2% to 5% (paid to the property advisor).
4. **Valuation Fee:** 0.5% (paid to the independent estate valuer to confirm the property value).
5. **Loan Processing / Admin Fee:** 1% (paid to the bank).
6. **Mortgage Insurance Premium:** 1% to 2% (covers death, disability, or default).

*Rule of Thumb:* Advise clients to save **an additional 10% to 12%** of the property purchase price to cover closing costs.

### Lesson 3: Preparing for the Lender Assessment

Once the numbers are validated, you must prepare your client for the bank's credit committee review:

#### Tips for the Client:
1. **Maintain Clean Bank Statements:** Do not bounce checks, do not exceed credit card limits, and avoid irregular cash transactions in the 3 months leading up to the application.
2. **Be Honest About Expenses:** The bank will cross-check bank statement withdrawals. If the client claims they spend ₦50,000 monthly but bank statements show ₦300,000 in monthly lifestyle spending, the lender will lose trust.
3. **Keep Existing Debt Low:** Advise the client to pay off credit card debts and personal loans before applying. This increases their available DTI capacity.
4. **Understand the Property:** Lenders will ask the buyer about the property's title and value. Ensure the buyer has reviewed the valuation report.

### The Mortgage Pre-Qualification Checklist
Before introducing a client to ULE Homes, Yalo, or any partner bank, ensure they tick all boxes:

- `[ ]` **Verifiable Income:** Client has 6 months of salary pay slips or audited business accounts.
- `[ ]` **Equity Contribution:** Client has at least 20% to 30% of the property value in cash.
- `[ ]` **Closing Cost Reserve:** Client has an additional 10% of the property value saved for fees.
- `[ ]` **Clean Credit Bureau Report:** No active defaults or bad debts recorded.
- `[ ]` **DTI Compliance:** Proposed monthly repayment is under 35% of net monthly income.
- `[ ]` **Clean Property Title:** The property being purchased has a C of O or Governor's Consent.

### Chapter Summary
- The Debt-to-Income (DTI) ratio is capped at 33% to 40% of net monthly income.
- Closing costs (Governor's consent, legal, agency, valuation, processing) require an additional 10% to 12% in cash.
- Property Advisors protect clients by running affordability calculations before contract signing.
- Prequalified clients have a 90%+ mortgage approval success rate.
